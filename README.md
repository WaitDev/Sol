# Sol
Wait Protocol Whitepaper
Every new Solana wallet faces the same first problem: you need SOL to do anything, but you need to do something to get SOL. Wait Protocol solves this for free.

Wait Protocol is a gasless, sustainable rewards platform that pays users in SOL simply for showing up. Think Sweatcoin, but for waiting: users visit wait.cash, wait, and earn with continuous rewards and perks that compound the longer they stay engaged. No purchase, no risk, no barrier to entry.

Beneath this simple consumer experience is real infrastructure: every claim helps onboard a new or existing wallet to Solana, distributes SOL directly to cover gas costs, and feeds a deflationary token economy ($WAIT) funded by trading activity rather than token inflation or user rewards, so the people who show up never create sell pressure on the asset that powers the ecosystem.

TOKEN ADOPTION
The acquisition of unique users will be facilitated by the Wait protocol; the platform's architecture naturally creates organic engagement through the network effect of sharing a platform that provides $SOL for free, especially when users incur gas fees. In addition to the base adoption layer, active marketing efforts, community events, giveaways, and our proprietary airdrop system will bring substantial traffic to the platform and ultimately expedite the adoption of $WAIT. Every user onboarded through the faucet is a new wallet that generates on-chain activity, driving trading volume, funds burns, and treasury growth. $WAIT is essentially a claim on the protocol’s growth engine, not a reward given to users, which is exactly why decoupling the two was the critical design decision. $WAIT reaches traders through two distinct channels: targeted dust airdrops to the most active on-chain Solana wallets existing traders who already generate volume and need no conversion and merit-based rewards to heavy protocol users who drive referrals and retention. The first channel drives trading volume directly; the second drives protocol engagement and word of mouth. Together they create the flywheel: more traders → more volume → more burns → tighter supply.

FUTURE REVENUE MODELS
The token tax establishes a sustainable early revenue foundation, but Wait Protocol is designed to layer additional revenue streams on top of it as the user base grows, not to replace fee-based income, but to compound it. The primary vehicle for this is platform monetization.

Every user onboarded through the faucet is a retained, active wallet on Solana. At scale, that user base becomes one of the most valuable distribution surfaces in the ecosystem, not because of token price, but because of daily active presence. A protocol reaching 10,000 daily active users represents over 300,000 monthly user touchpoints, a scale that commands serious attention from the projects, exchanges, and wallets competing for that audience.

Sponsored placements and partnerships are the natural monetization layer on top of that traffic. Exchanges seeking new depositors, wallets competing for default adoption, DeFi protocols launching to retail users, and infrastructure providers targeting active on-chain wallets are all natural buyers of placement within a high-DAU onboarding environment. A single mid-tier sponsorship from a brand like a major exchange or a Solana-native wallet, priced in line with comparable Web2 newsletter or app placements, represents meaningful incremental revenue that sits entirely outside the token tax mechanism.

SECURITY ARCHITECTURE
The Wait protocol reward pool is completely separated from the treasury and revenue wallet. This significantly reduces the potential loss of the reward pot; for context, the reward pot is filled from a hot wallet that is periodically funded by a cold wallet. Our architectural approach minimizes exposure by design; the reward pool is capped, isolated, and manually funded, meaning that, in any exploit scenario, maximum protocol exposure is limited to the active daily pot rather than the broader treasury. This represents a fundamentally different security posture than protocols with large attack-surface contracts; maximum loss is bounded by design, not by code quality alone.

REWARD SYSTEM & ANTI-BOT MECHANISMS
The Wait protocols reward system will be designed to be fair; bots will be punished and rewarded far less than real users. Users who have used Solana before or have on-chain transactional activity can earn rewards up to 5X-10X more than new users or bots! Other systems include our proprietary cluster bot detector, which reduces rewards for users’ multi-accounting. These systems are still in development, as the current early version of the protocol is still developing many of these features. Rewards are distributed equally at the moment so testing can be done properly. Beyond on-chain history, our cluster detection specifically targets funding-source patterns, grouping wallets from the same origin as a single actor. While behavioral timing analysis flags accounts claiming at suspiciously regular intervals, ensuring that simply aging a wallet or generating cheap transactions is not sufficient to game the reward system. New wallet funding is additionally capped at 5,000 wallets per day, a threshold derived from Solana’s estimated organic daily wallet creation of approximately 28,500 new addresses, representing roughly 17.5% of genuine new address activity. At $0.005 per claim, maximum daily exposure at this tier is $25, making coordinated bot attacks economically irrational before any technical detection fires. (This will be implemented in the future when operations of scale matter more heavily and are worth the investment) Underpinning all of the above is a simple economic reality: Wait Protocol operates two reward tiers, new wallets at $0.005 per claim and OG wallets with established on-chain history at $0.03 per claim. At the new wallet tier, a bot farm claiming across 1,000 wallets earns $5, trivially less than the operational cost of running the farm. At the OG tier, building wallets with sufficient on-chain history requires a minimum of ~1,000 transactions per wallet, costing approximately $0.25- $1 in Solana fees. This means an attacker needs extensive effort to make perfectly undetected claims just to break even before any infrastructure or proxy costs are factored in. The protocol is economically self-defending at both tiers. Attacking it costs more than it returns.
